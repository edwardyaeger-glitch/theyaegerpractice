About

The Yaeger Practice is a private psychotherapy practice based out of New York. We work with adults within and across three primary areas: life transitions; mood and emotional regulation; and sex and sexuality. The clients who arrive here are usually mid-shift in their lives - leaders, professionals, creators, change agents - and the work cuts across identity, vocation, relationships, and meaning (often all at once).

We treat therapy as a professional service, not a product. That means clinical rigor, ethical clarity, and a working posture that protects what therapy actually requires: privacy, time, autonomy, and a clinician's full attention.

The work is the same regardless of which door someone walks through. Different presenting concerns; same underlying methodologies.

How We Work

The work itself

Therapy is a professional service that requires education, training, supervision, licensure, and continuing education. There are field-based canons, frameworks, and regulatory bodies, just as there are in medicine, law, accounting, and other professions. We draw on counseling research and clinical experience, the kinds rooted in social science, natural science, and the humanities.

The work is the same system across the practice. Different presenting concerns; same underlying method.

Modality
Eclectic. Draws from various theoretical psychotherapeutic approaches rather than from just one.
Focus
Interpersonal. Hones in on resolving current, contemporaneous problems rather than deep-seated personality issues.
Scope
Holistic. Aims to treat the whole person (read: mind, body, and spirit) rather than just symptoms.
System
Integrative. Works within a robust and expanding ecosystem of mental health services, from psychiatric to somatic, from psychotropic to plant medicines.
Founder

About the founder, Edward J. Yaeger, Jr.

Edward Yaeger is a licensed mental health counselor in New York with graduate training and professional experience in counseling, business, public administration, and the humanities. That is an unusual combination. It is also the reason Edward tends to work well with leaders, executives, and agents of change whose transitions cut across identity, work, vocation, money, and meaning all at once.

Before becoming a therapist, Edward spent more than twenty-five years across startups and Fortune 100 companies in technology, finance, healthcare, media, entertainment, arts, politics, and education. He completed his clinical training during the pandemic, was licensed in 2023, and launched the practice the same year.
