Fees

Fees and insurance

I do not participate with insurance. Sessions are private-pay. What follows is the full structure, including the sliding scale, out-of-network reimbursement, and pre-tax options.

Rates

Session rates

Individual sessions are fifty minutes. Couples sessions are sixty minutes. These lengths are standard in private practice and are what the work requires. Insurance-based sessions are typically forty-five and fifty minutes, respectively.

The sliding scale is offered on the basis of self-reported need. No income verification is required. I trust clients to select the tier that honestly reflects their circumstances.

Included

What is included

  • A fifty-minute individual or sixty-minute couples session.
  • A monthly superbill, or a per-session superbill, for out-of-network reimbursement.
  • Cancellation and rescheduling up to one minute before the scheduled start, at no charge. The only fee that applies is for a no-show. The industry standard is a twenty-four to forty-eight hour window with a late-cancellation fee.
Out-of-Network

Out-of-network reimbursement

I do not bill insurance. Many PPO and POS plans reimburse a portion of out-of-network mental health care once the deductible is met, typically fifty to eighty percent of the plan's allowed amount. Reimbursement depends entirely on your plan.

On request, I provide a superbill, either monthly or per session, with everything your insurer requires to process a claim: NPI, dates of service, CPT codes, diagnostic codes, and the fee charged. You submit it to your insurer and they reimburse you directly.

Clients who prefer not to handle submission themselves may use Mentaya, a service I work with that files claims and tracks reimbursement on your behalf.

Under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act, a plan's out-of-network mental health benefits cannot be more restrictive than its out-of-network medical or surgical benefits. This is federal law.

A Good Faith Estimate is available under the No Surprises Act.

Pre-Tax Options

HSA, FSA, and the medical deduction

Therapy with a licensed mental health provider is a qualified medical expense under IRS rules. Clients with a Health Savings Account or Flexible Spending Account may use pre-tax dollars to pay for sessions, reducing the effective cost by the client's marginal tax rate.

Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of adjusted gross income are deductible on federal returns for clients who itemize. Therapy fees, net of any insurance reimbursement, count toward that threshold.

On the Transition

A note on the transition away from insurance

I recently made the decision to no longer accept in-network insurance clients, as insurance corporations and the health tech companies that enable them have been increasingly eroding both, access to and the quality of, healthcare services - while simultaneously exploiting and, in some cases, extorting healthcare service providers.

I understand the prohibitive costs of out-of-pocket, out-of-network, and/or fully paid healthcare services, as I am not just a provider but I am also a patient. It is something I have thought (and fought!) long and hard over, but to frustratingly negligible avail.

I already work with under-resourced clients at close to four times the industry average. As it stands at present, my conscience reflects this unfortunate decision, and vice versa.

Nevertheless, do let me know how I might be of assistance to you.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Why do you not accept insurance?
Protecting your privacy, your autonomy, and the integrity of the work is not a business decision. It is an ethical one. Third-party payers require a diagnosis on file, access to session-level data, and oversight of treatment length and modality. I declined to continue practicing under those terms.
How do I know what my insurance will reimburse?
Call the number on the back of your card and ask about out-of-network outpatient mental health benefits: whether your plan reimburses; the deductible; the allowed amount per session; the reimbursement percentage after deductible; and the annual visit limit. Reimbursement depends entirely on the plan.
How does the sliding scale work?
It is offered on the basis of self-reported need. There is no income verification and no application. I trust clients to select the tier that honestly reflects their circumstances, and to revisit that choice if circumstances change.
What is a superbill and what do I do with it?
A superbill is an itemized receipt with everything your insurer needs to process an out-of-network claim: NPI, dates of service, CPT codes, diagnostic codes, and the fee charged. I provide it monthly or per session. You submit it to your insurer and they reimburse you directly. I will provide a dedicated Mentaya hyperlink after I negotiate terms.
Can I use my HSA or FSA?
Yes. Therapy with a licensed mental health provider is a qualified medical expense under IRS rules. Clients with a Health Savings Account or Flexible Spending Account may pay for sessions with pre-tax dollars, which reduces the effective cost by the client's marginal tax rate.
What does a consultation cost?
Nothing. A brief, no-fee video consultation is offered to determine whether the practice is a suitable fit. It is not a clinical session and is not billed.
Is out-of-network reimbursement guaranteed?
No. Reimbursement depends on the specifics of your out-of-network benefits, your deductible, and your plan's allowed amount for the service code. The superbill makes reimbursement possible; it does not make it automatic. Calling the member services number on your insurance card before the first session is the fastest way to get a realistic estimate.
Do you raise fees for existing clients?
Rarely, and never without advance notice. If a rate change applies to ongoing clients, I give at least sixty days' notice and am open to conversation about timing or scale. Sliding-scale arrangements are revisited annually, or sooner if your circumstances change.
Do you offer packages, pre-payment discounts, or retainers?
No. Therapy is paid session by session. I do not sell bundles, multi-session discounts, or retainer arrangements. The sliding scale is the only fee flexibility I offer.
How do couples and polycules split the fee?
That is your decision. The fee is per session, not per person. Some clients split evenly, some split proportionally to income, some have one person cover the full cost. I do not require a particular arrangement and will not mediate it unless you want to bring it into the work.
