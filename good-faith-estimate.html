Good Faith Estimate

Your rights under the federal No Surprises Act

The Yaeger Practice · Edward J. Yaeger, Jr., LMHC
Effective date: September 1, 2023 · Last updated: January 1, 2026
You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate (GFE) explaining how much your medical and mental health care will cost. Under the federal No Surprises Act (effective January 1, 2022), healthcare providers must give uninsured and self-pay clients an estimate of the expected charges for medical services, including psychotherapy.

Who This Applies To

This notice applies to you if:

  • You do not have health insurance, or
  • You have insurance but do not plan to use it to pay for services at The Yaeger Practice.

The Yaeger Practice is a private-pay practice. We do not bill insurance directly, though we can provide superbills upon request for potential out-of-network reimbursement. A Good Faith Estimate will be provided to all self-pay clients.

Your Rights

  • You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of any non-emergency healthcare services, including psychotherapy.
  • You may request a Good Faith Estimate from any provider, in writing or verbally, before scheduling a service.
  • You will receive your Good Faith Estimate in writing at least 1 business day before your scheduled service.
  • If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you may dispute the bill through the federal Patient-Provider Dispute Resolution (PPDR) process.
  • Keep a copy of your Good Faith Estimate in a safe place in case you need it later.

What Your Estimate Will Include

ItemDescription
Your informationName, date of birth, contact information
Provider informationTreating clinician's name, license, and NPI
Service descriptionType of services (intake evaluation, individual psychotherapy, teletherapy)
Service codesStandard CPT/diagnosis codes where applicable
Estimated feesPer-session fee and an estimate of total annual cost based on expected frequency and duration
DisclaimersNoting that the estimate is not a contract and that actual charges may vary

Important Notes

  • The Good Faith Estimate shows the costs of items and services that are reasonably expected for your care. Actual services, needs, and costs may change over the course of treatment.
  • The estimate does not obligate you to receive services from The Yaeger Practice.
  • Length of treatment cannot always be predicted in advance; you and your therapist will review your care and goals regularly.
  • If you require services from additional providers (e.g., psychiatrist, physician), you may need to request separate Good Faith Estimates from them.

How to Request Your Estimate

You can request a Good Faith Estimate at any time before or during the intake process. We will automatically provide one in writing before your first session.

Disputing a Bill

If you are billed at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you have the right to dispute the bill. You may initiate a dispute with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services within 120 calendar days of receiving the bill. Starting a dispute does not affect the quality of healthcare services you receive.

For more information about your rights or the dispute process, visit www.cms.gov/nosurprises or call 1-800-985-3059.
